Cinnamon Raisin Bread

Description

This recipe is the first in a baking cook book my mom put together for me. This, like many of the other recipes in the book, is hand written. It's a sentimental for this reason. Cinnamon rasin bread will fill up the house with a warm and welcoming aroma. This recipe is a pleasure to make and a pleasure to eat.

The following is a list of the ingredients needed to bake this bread. You will see warm water on the list. This ingredient is essential in activating the yeast which is a living organism. You have to be careful to not overheat the water as this could kill the yeast.

Ingredients

  • one and one half cups of milk
  • one cup of warm water
  • two packets of active dry yeast
  • one cup of raisins
  • three large eggs
  • one and one fourth cups of sugar
  • a half cup of softened margarine
  • one teaspoon of salt
  • eight cups of all purpose flour
  • two tablespoons of milk
  • two tablespoons of cinnamon
  • and two tablespoons of melted butter

    • Steps

    1. warm milk in saucepan, remove from heat
  • bloom yeast in warm water in a large bowl
  • mix in raisins, eggs, a half cup of the sugar, and the margarine until it is combined.
  • stir in cooled milk and add flour and salt gradually
  • remove dough to a lightly floured surface
  • kneed for a few minutes, transfer to greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth and allow dough to rise for an hour
  • prepare three nine by five inch loaf pans
  • deflate risen dough, move to floured surface, roll into large rectangle, about half an inch thick
  • moisten top of dough with two tablespoons of milk
  • combine remaining sugar with cinnamon
  • sprinkle over moistened dough
  • roll up tightly into a log
  • cut large dough roll into thirds tuck under ends to form loaves
  • place into prepared loaf pans and lightly grease tops
  • cover and allow to rise once more, about an hour
  • preheat oven to 350 degrees F
  • bake until lightly browned and sounds hollow when tapped, about 45 minutes
  • remove loaves and brush with melted butter
